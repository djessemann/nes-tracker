what is this

a little music maker that sounds like an old nintendo. tap squares to place notes, press play to hear them loop, and export your song when you like it. everything happens in your browser, and your work is saved automatically on this device.

the four sounds

pulse 1 and pulse 2 — the bright, beepy melody sounds. the tone switch makes them thinner and buzzier or rounder and fuller.

triangle — soft and deep. good for basslines. it always plays at one loudness, just like on the real console.

noise — static, for drums. row 1 is short and crisp (think hi-hats), higher numbers get deeper and boomier.

placing notes

pick a sound, then tap squares. up and down is pitch, left to right is time. tap a note again to erase it. drag to the right to hold a note longer — long triangle notes make great basslines. each sound can only play one note at a time — a real limit of the console.

octave − / + shifts the grid higher or lower. notes you placed elsewhere keep playing even when they scroll out of view. undo takes back your last change, and placing a note plays it so you can hear what you're getting.

shaping the sound

each sound has a shape — how a note fades after it starts. pluck hits and dies away, lead holds strong, soft swells in gently, long fades slowly. tap edit to draw your own with your finger.

building a song

the loop | song switch at the top: loop plays just the part you're editing, over and over — good for sketching. song plays all your parts in a row.

in song view: + new adds an empty part, repeat plays the same part again (edit one, both change), copy makes a separate version you can change, delete removes one. tap a part to edit it.

saving

your song autosaves on this device. save downloads it as a small file you can keep or share; load opens one again — including songs from the older version of this app. nothing is stored online.

exporting

audio downloads your music as a sound file (wav). stems gives you a separate wav for each sound, zipped together, so you can mix them in other apps.

code is for programmers: it turns your song into a single file that plays in a real nes game — the music data plus a small player. how to wire it up is explained at the top of the file. if that means nothing to you, you can happily ignore this button.

not here yet

no slides, vibrato, or echo effects, and no sampled drums — those are on the list.
