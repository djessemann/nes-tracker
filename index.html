what is this

a music maker that sounds like an old nintendo. tap squares to place notes, press play to hear them, and export the result. everything runs in your browser and your work saves automatically on this device.

the four sounds

pulse 1 and pulse 2 — bright melody sounds. tone makes them thinner or fuller.

triangle — soft and deep, good for bass. it has no volume control; the real console doesn't either.

noise — static, for drums. row 1 is short and sharp; higher rows are deeper.

placing notes

pick a sound, then tap a square. up and down is pitch, left to right is time. tap a note to erase it. drag right to hold a note across steps. each sound plays one note at a time.

octave − / + moves the grid higher or lower. notes outside the visible octave still play. undo takes back the last change. clear erases the sound you're looking at.

shape

shape controls how each note fades. pluck fades fast, lead holds, soft starts quiet, long fades slowly. edit lets you draw your own.

building a song

loop plays the part you're editing, over and over. song plays all your parts in order.

in song view: + new adds an empty part. repeat plays the same part again — editing one changes both. copy makes a separate copy you can change. delete removes a part. tap a part to edit it.

saving

your song saves automatically on this device. save downloads it as a file; load opens one. files from the older version of this app still load.

exporting

audio downloads your music as a wav file. stems downloads one wav per sound, zipped.

code downloads the song as 6502 assembly for use in an nes game. instructions are at the top of the file. if that means nothing to you, ignore this button.
